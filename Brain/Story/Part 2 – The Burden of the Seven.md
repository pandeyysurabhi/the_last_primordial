# Part 2 – The Burden of the Seven
**Parent Volume**: [[Volume IV – The Core and the Final Choice]]

## Introduction
Purpose of this Part
This section is entirely emotional. The player already knows the truth about history; now they learn the truth about
the Seven themselves.
Instead of explaining what the Primordials did, this chapter shows what it cost them.
By the end of this part, [[Christ]] no longer sees them as gods.
He sees them as seven exhausted people who have carried reality on their shoulders for ten thousand years.

## Key Takeaways
• The Seven are revealed as ordinary humans who never stopped paying the price of their sacrifice.
• Each Primordial's "power" is shown to be an eternal burden rather than a privilege.
• [[Christ]] finally learns that his parents willingly accepted death to preserve the oldest law.
• The Seven spared [[Christ]] because they believed that only a person who could make their own choices
deserved to decide the future.

The endless cycle exists because every [[Choice Wielder]] inherited the weight of the past, making true freedom
impossible.

capable of making a truly independent decision.
