# 3. The Core Offers Three Paths
**Volume**: [[Volume IV – The Core and the Final Choice]] | **Part**: [[Part 3 – The Three Choices]]

---

[[The Core]] does not command [[Christ]].
Instead, it reveals three possibilities.

Not prophecies.
Not predetermined futures.
Only possibilities.
Each represents a different answer to the same question:
"How should reality move forward?"
Ending One – The Eternal Guardian
[[Christ]] accepts the burden of the Seven.
He absorbs the Fundamental Laws into himself.
The Seven are finally released from ten thousand years of responsibility.
For the first time, they become mortal again.
They grow old.
They laugh.
They cry.
They finally live the ordinary lives they sacrificed long ago.
[[Christ]] remains alone within [[The Core|the Core]].
Immortal.
Watching over reality.
His parents' sacrifice ultimately leads to humanity's survival.
But the price is absolute solitude.
He becomes the final guardian.
Theme
Self-sacrifice.
One life willingly bears the burden so that countless others may live freely.
Ending Two – The Age of Humanity
[[Christ]] destroys [[The Core|the Core]].
Without [[The Core|the Core]], the Fundamental Laws are no longer anchored by a central force.
Instead, they become part of the natural universe itself.
The Primordials disappear peacefully.
[[The Black Sword]] is gone forever.
Humanity is finally left without gods, guardians, or Chosen Ones.
The future becomes uncertain.
Wars may happen.
Peace may happen.
Great civilizations may rise.
Others may fall.
For the first time...
every future belongs entirely to humanity.
The age of divine intervention ends.
Theme
Freedom.
A future without destiny is both terrifying and beautiful.
Ending Three – The Broken Cycle (True Ending)
Instead of choosing either burden or destruction...
[[Christ]] chooses something no previous [[Choice Wielder]] imagined.
He separates responsibility.
The Seven remain.
But no longer alone.
Instead of seven eternal guardians...

the burden is shared among humanity itself.
Every generation willingly contributes a tiny fragment of itself to sustaining reality.
Not through blood.
Not through inheritance.
Through voluntary [[Choice|choice]].
Scientists.
Artists.
Farmers.
Teachers.
Children.
Anyone may one day choose to become a temporary Keeper of a Law.
No one serves forever.
No one inherits the burden.
No one is forced.
Reality survives because everyone carries a small responsibility instead of one person carrying everything.
The Seven finally step down.
Not because they are replaced.
But because humanity has finally matured enough to stand beside them.
The cycle ends.
Not through destruction.
Not through sacrifice.
Through cooperation.
Theme
Shared responsibility.
The future is strongest when no one is expected to carry it alone.
