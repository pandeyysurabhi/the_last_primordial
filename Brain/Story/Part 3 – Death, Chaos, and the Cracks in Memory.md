# Part 3 – Death, Chaos, and the Cracks in Memory
**Parent Volume**: [[Volume III – The Pilgrimage Through the Seven Realms]]

## Introduction
Purpose of this Part
This section marks the emotional midpoint of the game.
[[Christ]] enters the Realms of Death and Chaos, expecting to become stronger. Instead, both Realms attack something
far more dangerous than his body—his certainty.
By the end of this part, [[Christ]] no longer fully trusts his own memories.

## Key Takeaways
• The [[Realm of Death]] teaches that death itself is not evil—unresolved regret is.
• [[Morvex]]'s words hint that he never wanted [[Christ]]'s parents to die.
• The [[Realm of Chaos]] reinforces that true freedom requires both [[Choice|choice]] and responsibility.
• [[Xael]] subtly reveals the central theme of the game: choices matter because they are freely made, not
because they always change the outcome.
• [[The Black Sword]] reveals [[Christ]]'s first undeniable contradiction: his father once stood beside [[Morvex]] as an
ally.
