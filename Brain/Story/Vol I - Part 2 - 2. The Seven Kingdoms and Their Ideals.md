# 2. The Seven Kingdoms and Their Ideals
**Volume**: [[Volume I – The Birth of Reality]] | **Part**: [[Part 2 – The Collapse of Reality]]

---

Each kingdom approached [[The Core|the Core]] differently based on its own philosophy. Rather than seeking power, every
kingdom attempted to solve a problem that had troubled humanity for centuries.
[[Kingdom of Life]]
Its scholars believed no one should suffer from disease or physical weakness. Their research focused on
strengthening Life itself. They attempted to eliminate illness, aging and biological imperfection.
[[Kingdom of Death]]
This kingdom questioned why death had to exist at all. They believed grief was the greatest suffering known to
humanity. If death could simply be removed, every family could remain together forever.
[[Kingdom of Time]]
Its philosophers believed every disaster occurred because people lacked enough knowledge. If the future could be
observed accurately, wars, famankind, and tragedies could all be prevented before they happened.
[[Kingdom of Space]]
This kingdom dreamed of limitless exploration. No civilization would ever face resource shortages if new lands could
always be created. Distance itself became their enemy.
[[Kingdom of Creation]]
Its engineers believed anything humanity required should be possible to create. Food. Buildings. Machines. Entire
ecosystems. Nothing should remain impossible.
[[Kingdom of Destruction]]
Unlike the others, this kingdom viewed destruction as necessary. They argued that without controlled destruction,
nothing new could replace the old. Their research attempted to control destruction rather than eliminate it.
Ironically, this made them one of the few kingdoms that understood balance better than most.
[[Kingdom of Chaos]]
The smallest and most controversial kingdom believed perfection was dangerous. According to them, unpredictability
allowed evolution. Without Chaos, humanity would eventually stop growing. Their ideas were often ignored because
they opposed the dream of a perfectly ordered world.
Developer Note
Each kingdom genuinely believed they were helping humanity. This is important because later generations incorrectly
blame a single civilization for the catastrophe. History is more complicated than good versus evil.
