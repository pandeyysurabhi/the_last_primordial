# 4. The Seven Trials
**Volume**: [[Volume II – The Child of Choice]] | **Part**: [[Part 4 – The Seven Realms]]

---

[[Christ]] cannot simply walk into a Primordial's Realm.
Every Realm rejects those who are unworthy.
To enter, he must overcome the Realm Spirit.
Each Spirit tests more than combat ability.
Each challenges [[Christ]]'s understanding of the Law it represents.
Examples:
[[Realm of Time]]
The flow of time constantly changes.
Enemies grow older or younger during battle.
Destroyed bridges rebuild themselves.
Finished puzzles undo themselves.
The player must think beyond normal cause and effect.
[[Realm of Space]]
Distance becomes meaningless.
Rooms rearrange themselves.
Doors lead to unexpected places.
The player must navigate impossible geometry.
[[Realm of Life]]
The environment continuously grows.
Plants block paths.
Enemies regenerate.
The player must decide when preserving life creates greater suffering.
[[Realm of Death]]
The Realm is filled with lingering memories of those who have died.
Some help.
Some deceive.
Some simply refuse to move on.
The challenge is accepting loss rather than defeating it.
[[Realm of Creation]]
New structures appear constantly.
Weapons and enemies evolve during battle.
Nothing remains unfinished.
The player learns that endless creation without restraint eventually becomes overwhelming.
[[Realm of Destruction]]
Everything slowly crumbles.
Weapons break.

Platforms collapse.
The player learns that destruction is necessary—but excess destruction leaves nothing behind.
[[Realm of Chaos]]
Nothing behaves consistently.
Enemy patterns constantly change.
Controls occasionally shift.
Maps transform.
Random events become part of the gameplay itself.
The player must adapt instead of memorizing.
