GAME LORE BIBLE 
Volume I – The Birth of Reality 
Part 1 – The Beginning of Everything 
Purpose of this Volume 
This volume establishes the foundation of the game's universe. It explains how reality works, why the Seven 
Primordials exist, what the Core is, and how humanity unknowingly became responsible for the world's downfall. 
Understanding this history is essential before introducing Christ or the events of the game. 
1. The Beginning 
No one remembers how the universe was created. There are no myths, scriptures, or surviving records that explain 
where existence came from. Even the Seven Primordials do not know the answer. The only thing older than every 
star, every planet, and every living being is the Core. The Core is not a god. It has no emotions. No consciousness. No 
desires. It does not judge good or evil. It simply exists. If the universe is a machine, then the Core is its engine. If 
reality is a book, then the Core is the paper on which every page is written. Everything that exists ultimately depends 
on it. Destroying the Core would not merely destroy a civilization—it would erase the very rules that allow reality to 
function. 
2. The Core 
The Core is the source from which every fundamental law of existence emerged. It never created these laws 
intentionally. They formed naturally as reality stabilized. These laws became: 
• 
Creation  
• 
Destruction  
• 
Time  
• 
Space  
• 
Life  
• 
Death  
• 
Chaos  
These are not magical powers. They are the principles that allow reality to exist. Every moment, these laws work 
together. Life cannot exist without Death. Creation has no meaning without Destruction. Time cannot move without 
Space. Chaos allows change, preventing existence from becoming completely stagnant. If even one law disappears, 
reality begins to collapse. This balance is the foundation of the universe. 
Developer Note 
The Seven Primordials did not create these laws. They only became their guardians much later. This distinction is 
important because the Primordials are caretakers, not gods. 
3. Humanity's Rise 
Thousands of years passed. Life evolved. Eventually humanity became the dominant civilization. Unlike every species 
before it, humanity possessed curiosity. Instead of merely surviving, humans questioned everything around them. 
They built cities. Created kingdoms. Studied nature. Developed science. Recorded history. For the first time, a species 
attempted to understand reality itself. This curiosity became humanity's greatest strength. It would also become its 
greatest weakness. 
4. The Seven Kingdoms 
Human civilization expanded until seven major kingdoms dominated the world. Each kingdom developed its own 
identity.Instead of competing through constant warfare, they competed through progress. One kingdom became 
famous for scientific discoveries. Another mastered architecture. Another specialized in medicine. Others excelled in 
philosophy, navigation, engineering and military strategy. Although disagreements existed, this era is remembered as 
humanity's Golden Age. Knowledge spread freely. Technology advanced rapidly. The world experienced peace unlike 
anything seen before. 
Developer Note 
The Seven Kingdoms should each have their own visual identity. Different architecture, clothing, symbols and 
philosophies will make the world feel alive. Their differences also help explain why every kingdom later approaches 
the Core with a different goal. 
5. Discovery of the Core 
Everything changed during an archaeological expedition beneath the oldest mountain range in the world. Deep 
underground, explorers discovered ruins far older than humanity itself. At the center of those ruins rested the Core. 
No one understood what it was. No language could describe it.It emitted no light.No heat. No sound. Yet everyone 
who stood near it instinctively felt that it was older than existence itself. Years of research revealed something 
extraordinary. The Core responded to human interaction. Not through words. Not through commands. But through 
intent. Humanity had unknowingly discovered the foundation of reality. 
6. Humanity's Golden Age 
The Core transformed civilization. Diseases once thought incurable disappeared. Crop failures became rare. Natural 
disasters could be reduced. Previously impossible engineering became achievable. Life expectancy increased. Wars 
became less frequent. Humanity believed it had finally reached the beginning of a perfect future. People no longer 
feared nature. Instead... they believed they could improve it. At first, every use of the Core genuinely helped the 
world. The Seven Kingdoms celebrated together. The Core became humanity's greatest discovery. No one questioned 
whether there should be limits. After all... every decision seemed to improve life. 
7. The First Mistake 
History often describes humanity's downfall as greed. That is incorrect. Humanity did not begin by seeking power. It 
began by trying to eliminate suffering. One kingdom asked: "Can we cure every disease?" Another asked: "Can we 
prevent every natural disaster?" Another asked: "Can death itself be removed?" None of these questions were evil. 
Each was born from compassion. The problem was something humanity had not yet understood. Reality is not built 
from independent rules. Every law depends on every other. Removing one form of suffering often creates another. 
Changing one law changes countless others. The Core never refused these requests. It simply responded to 
humanity's intentions. And that was enough to begin breaking reality. 
End of Part 1 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Core predates the universe and is the foundation of reality.  
• 
The Seven Fundamental Laws existed before the Primordials.  
• 
The Primordials are guardians, not creators.  
• 
Humanity entered a Golden Age through knowledge and cooperation.  
• 
The discovery of the Core accelerated civilization beyond imagination.  
• 
Humanity's downfall began not from evil, but from the sincere desire to create a perfect world.  
• 
Every attempt to remove suffering unintentionally weakened the balance of reality, setting the stage for the 
collapse explored in Part 2.  
 
Part 2 – The Collapse of Reality 
Purpose of this Part 
This section explains how humanity unknowingly destabilized reality through the misuse of the Core, why the Seven 
Kingdoms were not villains despite causing the catastrophe, and how the world's collapse led to the birth of the 
Seven Primordials. 
1. Humanity Crosses the Line 
For decades after discovering the Core, humanity used it responsibly. 
The Core cured diseases, improved agriculture, prevented natural disasters, and accelerated scientific advancement. 
Every improvement reinforced the belief that humanity had finally gained control over its own future. 
Eventually, a dangerous question emerged. 
If the Core can change reality... what are its limits? 
No one intended to destroy the world. 
Instead, each kingdom believed they had discovered the next great step in human progress. 
The mistake was not ambition. 
The mistake was assuming reality could be perfected. 
2. The Seven Kingdoms and Their Ideals 
Each kingdom approached the Core differently based on its own philosophy. Rather than seeking power, every 
kingdom attempted to solve a problem that had troubled humanity for centuries. 
Kingdom of Life 
Its scholars believed no one should suffer from disease or physical weakness. Their research focused on 
strengthening Life itself. They attempted to eliminate illness, aging and biological imperfection. 
Kingdom of Death 
This kingdom questioned why death had to exist at all. They believed grief was the greatest suffering known to 
humanity. If death could simply be removed, every family could remain together forever. 
Kingdom of Time 
Its philosophers believed every disaster occurred because people lacked enough knowledge. If the future could be 
observed accurately, wars, famankind, and tragedies could all be prevented before they happened. 
Kingdom of Space 
This kingdom dreamed of limitless exploration. No civilization would ever face resource shortages if new lands could 
always be created. Distance itself became their enemy. 
Kingdom of Creation 
Its engineers believed anything humanity required should be possible to create. Food. Buildings. Machines. Entire 
ecosystems. Nothing should remain impossible. 
Kingdom of Destruction 
Unlike the others, this kingdom viewed destruction as necessary. They argued that without controlled destruction, 
nothing new could replace the old. Their research attempted to control destruction rather than eliminate it. 
Ironically, this made them one of the few kingdoms that understood balance better than most. 
Kingdom of Chaos 
The smallest and most controversial kingdom believed perfection was dangerous. According to them, unpredictability 
allowed evolution. Without Chaos, humanity would eventually stop growing. Their ideas were often ignored because 
they opposed the dream of a perfectly ordered world. 
Developer Note 
Each kingdom genuinely believed they were helping humanity. This is important because later generations incorrectly 
blame a single civilization for the catastrophe. History is more complicated than good versus evil. 
3. The Core Never Said "No" 
The Core possessed no morality. 
It could not distinguish between wisdom and foolishness. 
It simply responded to human intent. 
Every successful experiment encouraged larger experiments. 
Each kingdom modified a different fundamental law. 
Initially, the changes appeared harmless. 
Life expectancy increased. 
Agriculture flourished. 
Cities expanded. 
Weather became predictable. 
The world appeared to improve. 
However, reality itself was beginning to lose balance. 
4. The Collapse Begins 
The first abnormalities were subtle. 
Flowers bloomed during winter. 
Animals behaved unpredictably. 
The moon appeared slightly different every night. 
Scientists dismissed these events as harmless side effects. 
They were wrong. 
Soon the abnormalities spread across the world. 
Entire villages experienced sunrise and sunset within minutes. 
Some people remembered events that had never happened. 
Others forgot decades of their own lives overnight. 
Forests aged centuries within hours. 
Rivers flowed uphill. 
Some newborn children spoke languages that no longer existed. 
In other regions, death simply stopped working. 
People suffered fatal injuries but remained alive, trapped between life and death. 
Reality itself had begun contradicting its own rules. 
5. Understanding the Problem 
The greatest scholars eventually discovered the truth. 
The Seven Fundamental Laws were never independent systems. 
They constantly balanced one another. 
Strengthening one law weakened another. 
Removing Death disrupted Life. 
Altering Time affected Space. 
Unlimited Creation reduced the purpose of Destruction. 
Suppressing Chaos prevented adaptation. 
Reality functioned because every law depended on every other. 
The Core had not malfunctioned. 
It had obeyed humanity perfectly. 
Humanity had simply asked for the impossible. 
 
Developer Note 
The Core should never be portrayed as evil. 
It is comparable to gravity. 
Gravity is neither good nor bad. 
It simply exists. 
The Core functions in exactly the same way. 
6. The Final Council 
As reality deteriorated, the Seven Kingdoms abandoned their rivalries. 
Representatives from every nation gathered near the Core. 
This meeting later became known as The Final Council. 
Hundreds of proposals were discussed. 
Destroy the Core. 
Seal the Core. 
Reverse every experiment. 
Abandon civilization entirely. 
None worked. 
The damage had progressed beyond repair. 
Reality no longer possessed enough stability to heal itself. 
For the first time in history, humanity faced extinction. 
7. An Impossible Proposal 
Among countless scholars stood one researcher whose conclusion shocked the council. 
The Core could not repair reality. 
Only the Fundamental Laws themselves could. 
But the laws had no consciousness. 
No guardians. 
No one maintaining their balance. 
His proposal seemed absurd. 
If someone permanently fused themselves with each law... 
Reality might stabilize again. 
The cost was obvious. 
Those individuals would cease being human forever. 
No one volunteered. 
Until seven people stepped forward. 
None of them were rulers. 
None were chosen by prophecy. 
They simply believed that if someone had to bear eternity... 
it should be them. 
End of Part 2 
Key Takeaways 
• 
Humanity's downfall began with noble intentions rather than greed.  
• 
Each kingdom attempted to solve a genuine problem using the Core.  
• 
The Seven Fundamental Laws cannot be altered independently because they exist in perfect balance.  
• 
The Core faithfully obeyed humanity without judging whether its requests were wise.  
• 
The collapse of reality forced humanity to consider an irreversible solution.  
• 
Seven ordinary humans volunteered to sacrifice their humanity to preserve existence.  
 
Part 3 – The Seven Primordials 
Purpose of this Part 
This section explains how the Seven Volunteers became the Primordials, the sacrifices they made, the responsibilities 
they inherited, and why they should never be viewed as gods or villains. It also establishes the emotional foundation 
of the story by showing that the Primordials are victims of their own choices. 
1. The Seven Volunteers 
The proposal made during the Final Council required seven individuals to permanently merge with the Seven 
Fundamental Laws. 
This was not a ritual that granted power. 
It was a sacrifice. 
The volunteers would lose their mortality, individuality, and freedom. Their lives would become inseparably linked to 
the law they inherited. If that law weakened, they would weaken. If reality suffered, they would suffer with it. 
The council did not force anyone to accept this burden. 
Seven people chose it willingly. 
History no longer remembers their original names. 
Over thousands of years, they became known only by the names of the laws they carried. 
• 
Aetherion – Guardian of Creation  
• 
Nihros – Guardian of Destruction  
• 
Chronar – Guardian of Time  
• 
Vastara – Guardian of Space  
• 
Elaria – Guardian of Life  
• 
Morvex – Guardian of Death  
• 
Xael – Guardian of Chaos  
 
Developer Note 
The Primordials were ordinary humans before the sacrifice. 
This is important because the player should eventually realize that the Seven are not divine beings—they are 
ordinary people who made the greatest sacrifice in history. 
2. The Ascension 
The Seven entered the Core together. 
No one outside the chamber witnessed what happened. 
The process lasted only a few moments. 
When the chamber finally opened, the seven volunteers emerged transformed. 
Their bodies no longer aged. 
Their physical forms had become manifestations of their respective laws. 
Reality immediately began stabilizing. 
Time resumed its natural flow. 
Space repaired itself. 
Life and Death returned to balance. 
The universe had survived. 
Humanity celebrated. 
The Seven did not. 
They immediately understood the true cost of what had happened. 
3. The Price of Becoming a Primordial 
The Seven had not gained immortality. 
They had lost the ability to live. 
Each Primordial now experienced existence differently. 
Chronar – Time 
Chronar experiences every moment simultaneously. 
For him, the past, present and future all exist at once. 
He remembers conversations that have not happened yet. 
He mourns deaths that are still decades away. 
He can never truly experience surprise. 
Vastara – Space 
Vastara constantly maintains the structure of reality. 
Every tear in space causes immense pain. 
She cannot rest. 
Even while speaking to others, part of her consciousness is repairing reality across the universe. 
Elaria – Life 
Elaria feels every living being. 
Every birth. 
Every illness. 
Every heartbeat. 
Every act of love. 
Every moment of suffering. 
She experiences all of it simultaneously. 
Saving one life means witnessing thousands of others die elsewhere. 
Morvex – Death 
Morvex experiences every death. 
Every final breath. 
Every goodbye. 
Every parent losing a child. 
Every child losing a parent. 
He is not responsible for causing death. 
He simply bears witness to every single one. 
Over thousands of years, grief becomes his constant companion. 
etherion – Creation 
Creation never ends. 
Stars are born. 
Worlds form. 
New life appears constantly. 
Aetherion's responsibility is to ensure creation never overwhelms reality. 
Without destruction, endless creation would eventually consume existence. 
Nihros – Destruction 
Unlike humanity's beliefs, destruction is not evil. 
Old stars must die for new ones to exist. 
Dead forests allow new ecosystems to grow. 
Broken civilizations create opportunities for better ones. 
Nihros ensures destruction remains balanced rather than excessive. 
He is hated for a responsibility that reality cannot survive without. 
Xael – Chaos 
Chaos is not disorder. 
Chaos is possibility. 
Xael maintains uncertainty itself. 
Without Chaos, every event would become predetermined. 
Nothing new could ever happen. 
Creativity. 
Evolution. 
Choice. 
All require a degree of chaos. 
Among the Seven, Xael carries perhaps the most misunderstood responsibility. 
Developer Note 
Each Primordial's suffering should influence their visual design. 
For example: 
• 
Chronar may appear exhausted despite being immortal.  
• 
Morvex should look emotionally distant rather than evil.  
• 
Elaria should appear compassionate but constantly overwhelmed.  
• 
Xael's appearance should subtly change over time, reflecting endless possibilities.  
The player's first impression should never be "monster." 
It should be "someone carrying an impossible burden." 
4. The First Thousand Years 
Reality stabilized. 
Civilizations recovered. 
Humanity rebuilt. 
The Seven quietly continued their responsibilities. 
They never demanded worship. 
They never ruled kingdoms. 
They rarely interfered with politics. 
Their purpose was only to maintain the balance of reality. 
Over generations, the truth began disappearing. 
The people who remembered the Seven as human eventually died. 
Their descendants knew only stories. 
Stories became myths. 
Myths became religion. 
Temples were built. 
Statues were carved. 
Prayers replaced gratitude. 
The Seven watched silently as humanity transformed them into gods. 
Correcting history served no purpose. 
Whether humanity feared them or worshipped them... 
their responsibilities remained the same. 
5. A Difficult Truth 
 
As centuries passed, the Seven noticed something deeply troubling. 
Humanity had learned nothing. 
The Core had been sealed away, yet human nature remained unchanged. 
Kingdoms still fought wars. 
People still sought power. 
Civilizations still believed they alone possessed the correct vision for the future. 
The Seven realized that if the Core were ever discovered again, history would repeat itself. 
The problem had never been the Core. 
The problem was that humanity always believed it could perfectly control reality. 
6. The Missing Law 
While studying the balance of existence, the Seven made a shocking discovery. 
Reality did not originally consist of only seven laws. 
There had once been another. 
One that no longer existed among them. 
A law unlike the others. 
One that no guardian had inherited. 
One that could not be predicted. 
One that could not be controlled. 
It was known only as... 
Choice. 
Unlike Creation or Time, Choice refused to remain constant. 
It existed wherever free will existed. 
It could not be permanently assigned to a single guardian. 
It rejected permanence itself. 
The Seven now understood why history continued repeating. 
As long as Choice existed... 
the possibility of another catastrophe would always remain. 
End of Part 3 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Seven became guardians, not rulers.  
• 
Immortality became an eternal punishment rather than a reward.  
• 
Each Primordial experiences constant suffering directly tied to their law.  
• 
Humanity gradually forgot the truth and began worshipping the Seven as gods.  
• 
The Seven realized humanity's greatest weakness was not greed, but the belief that reality could be 
perfected.  
• 
They discovered the existence of an Eighth Law—Choice—which could never be permanently contained. 
 
Part 4 – The Forgotten Law 
Purpose of this Part 
This section concludes the history of the ancient world. It explains why the Seven Primordials eventually discovered 
the existence of an Eighth Fundamental Law, why they could never become its guardians, and how their attempt to 
preserve reality unintentionally began a cycle that would continue for thousands of years. This volume ends just 
before the birth of Christ. 
1. The Eighth Law 
For centuries, the Seven believed reality was governed by only seven fundamental laws. 
Creation. 
Destruction. 
Time. 
Space. 
Life. 
Death. 
Chaos. 
Everything appeared balanced. 
Yet something continued to trouble them. 
Humanity always made different decisions. 
History never repeated itself exactly. 
Even Chronar, who experienced every timeline simultaneously, occasionally encountered possibilities that could not 
be predicted. 
Something existed beyond Time itself. 
2. The Discovery 
After centuries of studying the Core, the Seven reached an impossible conclusion. 
Reality contained an eighth law. 
Unlike every other law, it possessed no constant form. 
It could not be measured. 
It could not be stabilized. 
It could not be inherited. 
It existed wherever free will existed. 
The Seven named it... 
Choice. 
Unlike the other laws, Choice refused to belong to anyone. 
It constantly changed with every decision made by every living being. 
Developer Note 
Choice is not "good." 
It is also not "evil." 
Choice is simply the possibility that reality may take a different path. 
Without Choice, progress cannot exist. 
Without Choice, mistakes also cannot exist. 
3. Why Choice Could Never Have A Guardian 
 
The Seven initially believed someone could simply inherit Choice like the other laws. 
Every attempt failed. 
Unlike Time or Death... 
Choice rejected permanence. 
Whenever someone tried to bind it, 
it disappeared. 
Whenever someone tried to control it, 
it resurfaced somewhere else. 
The Seven realized something disturbing. 
Choice wasn't meant to be owned. 
It was meant to be experienced. 
4. The Cycle Begins 
Although Choice could not remain inside one guardian forever, 
it did leave traces. 
Occasionally, 
one individual would unknowingly become connected to the forgotten law. 
These individuals later became known as... 
Choice Wielders. 
They were not selected because they were powerful. 
They were selected because they stood at moments capable of changing history. 
Each eventually found the same ancient weapon. 
A black sword unlike anything created by humanity. 
No one knew who forged it. 
Not even the Seven. 
The sword appeared wherever the next Choice Wielder emerged. 
5. The Black Sword 
The Black Sword did not grant power. 
It granted perspective. 
Every Choice Wielder left behind their memories inside the blade. 
When the next Wielder inherited it, 
they inherited every previous failure. 
Every regret. 
Every philosophy. 
Every unanswered question. 
The sword became less of a weapon... 
and more of a conversation spanning thousands of years. 
Some voices believed humanity deserved another chance. 
Others believed the Seven should rule forever. 
Some argued the Core should be destroyed. 
Others believed the cycle itself was necessary. 
No Choice Wielder ever agreed completely with those who came before. 
Developer Note 
The Black Sword should function almost like a library. 
Instead of simply speaking, 
different previous Wielders should occasionally disagree with one another. 
This makes the sword feel alive without actually being sentient. 
6. The Seven's Decision 
The Seven never hunted the Choice Wielders. 
They observed them. 
Each time, 
they hoped someone would discover a solution they themselves had overlooked. 
Every Choice Wielder eventually reached the same conclusion. 
Reality could not continue like this. 
But none could agree on how to save it. 
Some died protecting humanity. 
Some died opposing the Seven. 
Some disappeared completely. 
History remembered none of them. 
The cycle continued. 
Again. 
Again. 
Again. 
For nearly ten thousand years. 
7. The Burden of Hope 
The Seven slowly stopped believing the cycle would ever end. 
Every Choice Wielder possessed courage. 
Many possessed wisdom. 
None possessed the answer. 
The Primordials began to accept a painful truth. 
Perhaps reality was never meant to be perfect. 
Perhaps existence itself required uncertainty. 
Yet they continued waiting. 
Not because they expected success. 
But because hope was the only thing they still possessed. 
End of Volume I 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Seven discover the existence of an Eighth Law called Choice.  
• 
Choice cannot become a permanent Fundamental Law because it rejects ownership.  
• 
Choice repeatedly manifests through different individuals across history.  
• 
Every Choice Wielder inherits the memories of previous Wielders through the Black Sword.  
• 
The Seven do not view Choice Wielders as enemies; they see them as humanity's final hope.  
• 
For ten thousand years, every Choice Wielder fails to end the cycle.  
 
Volume II – The Child of Choice 
 
Part 1 – The Impossible Child 
 
Purpose of this Part 
This volume shifts the focus from the history of the world to the beginning of the protagonist's story. 
It explains who Christ's parents were, why they broke the oldest law of reality, why the Seven Primordials were 
forced to execute them, and why Christ became the most important person in history before he was even old enough 
to understand the world. 
This section should make one thing clear: 
Christ was never destined to save the world. He was born because two people believed the world deserved 
another chance. 
 
1. The Endless Cycle 
For nearly ten thousand years, the cycle repeated. 
A Choice Wielder would appear. 
The Black Sword would find them. 
They would learn the truth about reality. 
They would eventually confront the Seven Primordials. 
And... 
they would fail. 
Some believed humanity deserved complete freedom. 
Others believed the Primordials should permanently rule over reality. 
Some tried destroying the Core. 
Others attempted to control it. 
Every solution solved one problem while creating another. 
The cycle always began again. 
The Seven never interfered unless absolutely necessary. 
Instead... 
they watched. 
Every Choice Wielder represented another possibility. 
Another hope. 
Another failure. 
2. The Last Choice Wielder 
The final Choice Wielder before Christ was different. 
Unlike his predecessors, he never saw the Primordials as enemies. 
Instead of searching for a way to defeat them, he spent years understanding them. 
He observed Chronar's endless isolation. 
He witnessed Morvex experience every death across existence. 
He watched Elaria mourn countless lives she could never save. 
He realized something no previous Wielder had fully accepted. 
The Seven were prisoners. 
They had sacrificed everything to save humanity... 
and humanity remembered them only as gods or monsters. 
For the first time in history, a Choice Wielder questioned whether the Seven needed saving just as much as humanity 
did. 
Developer Note 
The Last Choice Wielder should never be portrayed as a failed hero. 
Instead, he is a philosopher. 
He asks questions no one else has considered. 
His greatest strength is not his combat ability. 
It is his understanding of people. 
 
3. Morvex's Greatest Mistake 
Among the Seven, Morvex carried the heaviest burden. 
Every death in existence passed through him. 
He watched children lose parents. 
Parents lose children. 
Friends lose one another. 
Empires disappear. 
Stars collapse. 
He understood loss better than anyone. 
Over thousands of years... 
he became emotionally attached to a mortal woman. 
She never feared death. 
She accepted it as a natural part of life. 
That acceptance fascinated Morvex. 
For the first time since becoming a Primordial... 
he allowed himself to love someone. 
When her natural life ended... 
Morvex broke reality's oldest principle. 
Instead of allowing her soul to move on... 
he prevented her death. 
She became immortal. 
Not a Primordial. 
Not a goddess. 
Simply... 
a human who could no longer die. 
 
4. Judgment of the Seven 
The other Primordials immediately discovered what Morvex had done. 
This was the first time one of them had used their authority for personal reasons. 
The consequences could have been catastrophic. 
If Primordials began changing reality according to emotion... 
they would become exactly what humanity once tried to become. 
Rulers of existence. 
The Seven gathered. 
For the first time in thousands of years... 
they argued with one another. 
Some demanded punishment. 
Others understood Morvex's loneliness. 
In the end... 
they reached a unanimous decision. 
The woman would be allowed to live. 
But Morvex would never again interfere with the natural cycle for personal reasons. 
The incident was sealed from history. 
Only the Seven remembered it. 
 
Developer Note 
This decision is important. 
The Seven are not emotionless. 
They are capable of compassion. 
Their greatest strength is also their greatest weakness. 
They understand suffering because they constantly experience it themselves.  
 
5 . An Impossible Love 
Centuries later... 
the immortal woman met the Last Choice Wielder. 
He already knew her history. 
She knew who he really was. 
Neither approached the other because of destiny. 
They simply found someone capable of understanding a burden that no ordinary person could. 
Their relationship grew slowly. 
Not through adventure. 
Not through prophecy. 
Through conversations. 
Together... 
they discussed every previous Choice Wielder. 
Every failed solution. 
Every sacrifice. 
Every mistake humanity had repeated throughout history. 
Eventually... 
they arrived at a terrifying realization. 
The cycle itself was the problem. 
Every Choice Wielder entered the story too late. 
They inherited thousands of years of failure. 
By the time they understood reality... 
the cycle had already claimed another victim. 
 
6. The Oldest Law 
Long before history began... 
the Seven established one absolute law. 
No bearer of a Fundamental Law may pass their burden through blood. 
The reason was simple. 
The burden of reality must always be accepted willingly. 
Never inherited. 
If Primordials could have children... 
those children would inherit unimaginable responsibility without ever choosing it. 
The Seven believed forcing eternity upon someone was the greatest injustice imaginable. 
This became the oldest law in existence. 
No exceptions. 
 
7. A Different Solution 
The Last Choice Wielder began questioning this law. 
Not because he wanted power. 
Not because he wanted a family. 
Because he believed every previous Choice Wielder had begun their journey too late. 
What if... 
instead of waiting for Choice to appear... 
someone was born carrying its essence? 
Someone who grew up questioning reality instead of discovering it halfway through life. 
Someone capable of breaking the cycle before repeating it. 
The immortal woman understood the consequences immediately. 
If they had a child... 
they would violate the oldest law in existence. 
The Seven would have no choice but to intervene. 
 
8. The Birth of Christ 
Despite knowing the consequences... 
they made their decision. 
Not out of selfishness. 
Not out of rebellion. 
But because they believed the future deserved one final chance. 
Months later... 
a child was born. 
They named him... 
Christ. 
Nothing extraordinary happened that day. 
The sky remained clear. 
The earth did not tremble. 
No prophecy announced his arrival. 
To the world... 
he was simply another child. 
Only one thing was different. 
For the first time in ten thousand years... 
Choice had not selected its next Wielder. 
Choice had been born. 
 
End of Part 1 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Last Choice Wielder understood that the Primordials were victims, not villains.  
• 
Morvex's love for a mortal woman created the first crack in the ancient laws.  
• 
The Seven chose mercy instead of punishment, showing that they remained compassionate despite their 
burdens.  
• 
The Last Choice Wielder and the immortal woman concluded that the endless cycle itself was the true 
enemy.  
• 
They deliberately broke the oldest law by having a child.  
• 
Christ became the first person in history to be born with the essence of Choice, making his existence 
unprecedented and unknowingly setting the stage for the greatest crisis the Seven would ever face.  
 
Part 2 – The Judgment of the Seven 
 
Purpose of this Part 
This section explains how the Seven Primordials discovered Christ's existence, why they were forced to execute his 
parents despite understanding their intentions, and how the Black Sword ultimately reached Christ. 
This is the emotional turning point that creates the central conflict of the game. 
 
1. Reality Notices the Impossible 
The birth of Christ remained hidden from humanity. 
But it could not remain hidden from reality. 
The Fundamental Laws are interconnected. 
Whenever one of them changes unnaturally, every other law feels the disturbance. 
Chronar saw a future that had never existed before. 
Vastara sensed a distortion spreading through space. 
Elaria felt a new life unlike any she had ever experienced. 
Morvex realized that the immortal woman had given birth. 
The Seven immediately understood. 
The oldest law had been broken. 
 
2. The Council of the Seven 
For the first time in centuries, the Seven gathered. 
No enemies stood before them. 
No catastrophe threatened reality. 
Only a child. 
The discussion lasted longer than any decision they had made since becoming Primordials. 
Chronar 
He had already seen countless possible futures. 
Some ended with Christ saving both humanity and the Seven. 
Others ended with reality collapsing completely. 
Even Time could not determine which future would become real. 
Morvex 
He accepted full responsibility. 
He admitted that his decision, thousands of years ago, had made this moment possible. 
He pleaded for mercy. 
Not for himself... 
but for the child. 
Elaria 
She believed no child should suffer for the choices of their parents. 
She proposed allowing Christ to grow up as an ordinary human under the Seven's protection. 
Nihros 
He reminded everyone that reality could not survive if laws became optional. 
The oldest law had existed for ten thousand years. 
Breaking it without consequence would destroy everything the Seven had sacrificed to preserve. 
Xael 
Chaos offered no answer. 
Only possibilities. 
He argued that perhaps this impossible child represented the change reality had been waiting for. 
Aetherion 
Creation always demanded hope. 
He believed humanity deserved another opportunity. 
Yet hope without responsibility had caused the first catastrophe. 
Vastara 
She remained silent for most of the meeting. 
Finally she spoke. 
"If we decide with our hearts instead of our responsibilities... 
we become no different from those who nearly destroyed reality." 
Silence filled the chamber. 
No one disagreed. 
 
Developer Note 
This meeting is extremely important. 
The player should understand that every Primordial genuinely wants to spare Christ's family. 
Their decision is born from duty, not hatred. 
 
3. The Verdict 
The Seven reached a unanimous decision. 
The law itself could not be broken. 
Christ's parents had knowingly violated the oldest rule of reality. 
If they survived... 
every future Law Bearer could justify breaking the same law. 
The balance they had protected for ten thousand years would begin to collapse. 
The sentence was final. 
Both parents would die. 
The child would live. 
 
4. Why Christ Was Spared 
The Seven never intended to punish Christ. 
He had committed no crime. 
He had never chosen to be born. 
Executing him would contradict the very principle the oldest law existed to protect. 
Children should never bear responsibility for choices they did not make. 
Instead... 
the Seven decided to observe him. 
If he truly carried the essence of Choice... 
his own decisions would determine the future. 
Not theirs. 
 
5. The Final Night 
Christ's parents knew the Seven were coming. 
They did not run. 
They did not fight. 
They had expected this day from the moment Christ was conceived. 
That night... 
they placed their sleeping son in his bed. 
His mother kissed his forehead. 
His father looked at him one final time. 
Neither regretted their decision. 
They believed they had already succeeded. 
Not because they had changed the world... 
but because they had given it a possibility that had never existed before. 
 
6. The Execution 
The Seven descended upon the village. 
No battle took place. 
No innocent people were harmed. 
The Primordials did not come as conquerors. 
They came as executioners carrying out a sentence they wished had never been necessary. 
Christ's parents accepted their fate. 
Before his final breath... 
Christ's father entrusted the Black Sword to reality itself. 
Rather than leaving it behind, 
he released it from his own control. 
The sword disappeared. 
Waiting... 
for the day Christ would be ready to inherit it. 
The Seven carried out the sentence. 
By sunrise... 
both parents were gone. 
 
7. A Child's Memory 
Christ was too young to understand what had happened. 
His mind could not process the truth. 
Instead... 
his memories became fragmented. 
Over the years... 
they transformed into recurring nightmares. 
A burning house. 
A black sword. 
His parents calling his name. 
Seven towering figures watching from the sky. 
He never knew whether those visions were dreams... 
or forgotten memories. 
 
End of Part 2 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Seven discover Christ through disturbances in the Fundamental Laws. 
• 
They debate his fate rather than immediately enforcing the law. 
• 
Every Primordial has a different perspective, highlighting their personalities. 
• 
Christ is spared because the Seven refuse to punish a child for his parents' choices. 
• 
His parents willingly accept their execution, believing Christ represents humanity's final hope. 
• 
Before dying, Christ's father releases the Black Sword, ensuring it will one day find Christ. 
• 
Christ grows up haunted by fragmented memories that become the game's opening nightmare. 
 
 
Part 3 – The Black Sword and the Journey Begins 
Purpose of this Part 
This section marks the beginning of the actual game. 
It explains Christ's life after his parents' death, his recurring nightmares, how the Black Sword chooses him, and why 
he sets out on his journey. This is also where the player's motivation is established: revenge against the Seven 
Primordials. 
Although the player believes the Seven are the villains at this point, the document should make it clear that the 
player only knows Christ's perspective, not the complete truth. 
 
1. A Life Built on Fragments 
After his parents' execution, Christ survived because the Seven intentionally left him alive. 
To the outside world, he was simply another orphan whose parents had died under mysterious circumstances. 
No one knew the truth. 
The village elders raised him. 
They never spoke about his parents. 
Whenever Christ asked how they died, he received different answers. 
"They died protecting the village." 
"They were taken by monsters." 
"It was an accident." 
Nobody lied out of cruelty. 
Nobody actually knew. 
The Seven had erased every direct witness to protect the truth from spreading. 
As a result, Christ grew up with questions that no one could answer. 
 
2. The Nightmares 
From early childhood, Christ experienced the same nightmare repeatedly. 
He stood inside a burning home. 
A black sword rested in his hands. 
His parents called his name. 
Seven enormous figures watched silently from the sky. 
Every dream ended before he could understand what had happened. 
The older he became... 
the more vivid the dreams became. 
Sometimes he woke with tears despite remembering nothing. 
Sometimes he woke gripping an invisible sword. 
Sometimes... 
he heard voices. 
Whispers. 
Names. 
Fragments of conversations. 
He believed he was losing his mind. 
 
Developer Note 
The nightmares should evolve throughout the game. 
Early nightmares are confusing and symbolic. 
Later nightmares gradually reveal actual memories. 
This allows players to slowly piece together the truth alongside Christ. 
 
3. An Ordinary Boy 
Despite his nightmares, Christ lived an ordinary life. 
He helped villagers. 
Worked in the fields. 
Learned basic swordsmanship from the village guard. 
Spent evenings listening to stories from travelers. 
He laughed. 
Made friends. 
Dreamed about leaving the village one day. 
Nothing about him suggested he would become the next Choice Wielder. 
This contrast is important. 
The player should first know Christ as a normal human before witnessing his transformation. 
 
4. The Return of the Black Sword 
Far away... 
hidden beneath the ruins of an ancient civilization... 
the Black Sword waited. 
For years it remained motionless. 
It rejected everyone who attempted to claim it. 
Kings. 
Mercenaries. 
Treasure hunters. 
None could even lift it. 
Then... 
without warning... 
the sword disappeared. 
At sunrise... 
it appeared before Christ. 
No explosion. 
No divine light. 
It simply stood upright outside his home. 
Waiting. 
Christ approached cautiously. 
The moment his hand touched the hilt... 
the world fell silent. 
 
5. The Voices Within 
Christ did not gain power immediately. 
Instead... 
he heard voices. 
Dozens. 
Hundreds. 
Then thousands. 
Each belonged to a previous Choice Wielder. 
None spoke at once. 
Instead, they argued. 
One believed humanity should never have touched the Core. 
Another insisted the Primordials had ruled for too long. 
Some regretted their choices. 
Others stood by them. 
One voice laughed. 
Another cried. 
Some begged Christ to abandon the sword. 
Others urged him to continue the fight they could not finish. 
The sword did not command him. 
It offered perspectives. 
Every Choice Wielder had left behind their memories, doubts, and beliefs. 
Christ realized that he was no longer alone. 
 
Developer Note 
The previous Wielders should never function as quest markers or tutorial characters. 
Think of them as philosophers. 
Sometimes they help. 
Sometimes they contradict one another. 
Occasionally, they are completely wrong. 
The player should decide whose ideas to trust. 
 
6 . The First Memory 
The moment Christ accepted the sword... 
his recurring nightmare changed. 
For the first time... 
it continued beyond the point where it had always ended. 
He saw his parents clearly. 
He saw towering silhouettes standing over them. 
He heard one final sentence before waking. 
"Forgive us... one day, you'll understand." 
Christ misunderstood those words. 
He believed his parents were asking forgiveness because they had failed to protect him. 
In reality... 
they were asking forgiveness for the burden they had placed upon him. 
This misunderstanding becomes the emotional foundation of Christ's hatred toward the Seven. 
 
7. The Decision 
The Black Sword did not force Christ to leave. 
It simply revealed fragments of the truth. 
Someone had murdered his parents. 
Someone powerful enough to erase the truth from history. 
The previous Wielders knew the answer. 
One name appeared repeatedly within their memories. 
The Seven Primordials. 
Christ reached the only conclusion that made sense to him. 
The Seven had destroyed his family. 
He would destroy them in return. 
He packed only the essentials. 
The Black Sword. 
A small bag of supplies. 
A pendant that once belonged to his mother. 
Without telling anyone... 
he walked out of the village. 
He believed he was beginning a journey of revenge. 
In reality... 
he had just stepped into a journey designed to challenge everything he believed. 
 
8. The First Guide 
As Christ crossed the village border, one voice within the Black Sword became clearer than the others. 
It belonged to the Last Choice Wielder—his father. 
However, Christ didn't recognize it. 
The voice refused to reveal its identity. 
Instead, it gave only one warning: 
"Hatred makes decisions easy. Truth makes them difficult." 
Before Christ could ask another question... 
the voice disappeared. 
He dismissed it as another hallucination. 
But from that moment onward, his father's spirit quietly watched over him, choosing not to interfere unless 
absolutely necessary. 
 
End of Part 3 
Key Takeaways 
• 
Christ grows up without knowing the truth about his parents' deaths. 
• 
His nightmares are fragmented memories rather than ordinary dreams. 
• 
The Black Sword chooses Christ when he is ready, carrying the wisdom and disagreements of every previous 
Choice Wielder. 
• 
Christ misinterprets his parents' final words, strengthening his desire for revenge. 
• 
Believing the Seven Primordials murdered his family, Christ leaves his village with one goal: to kill them. 
• 
The spirit of the Last Choice Wielder (his father) is present within the Black Sword but keeps his identity 
hidden, guiding Christ only through subtle advice rather than direct answers. 
 
Part 4 – The Seven Realms 
Purpose of this Part 
This section concludes the prologue of the game. It introduces the concept of the Seven Realms, explains why the 
Primordials created them, why Christ must clear them in a specific order, and establishes the true objective of the 
game's progression. 
By the end of this part, the player should understand one thing: 
The Seven are not trying to stop Christ. They are trying to determine whether he deserves to learn the truth. 
 
1. The World Beyond the Physical 
Reality exists on two different levels. 
The first is the Mortal Realm, where humanity lives. 
The second consists of the Seven Realms, each representing one Fundamental Law. 
These Realms are not planets or dimensions created by magic. 
They are manifestations of reality itself. 
Every Primordial is connected to one Realm. 
If a Realm collapses, the corresponding Law weakens. 
If a Law weakens, reality itself begins to fracture. 
 
Developer Note 
The Seven Realms should each have a completely different visual identity. Players should instantly recognize which 
Law they are inside without needing dialogue. 
 
2. Why the Realms Exist 
When the Seven became Primordials, they realized something dangerous. 
The Fundamental Laws could not simply exist within themselves. 
They needed places where those laws could remain stable. 
To solve this, each Primordial shaped a Realm around their Law. 
These Realms became anchors for reality. 
The Primordials do not rule these Realms like kings. 
Instead, they maintain them. 
They spend almost all of their existence ensuring that their Realm remains balanced. 
This is why they rarely interact with humanity. 
Leaving a Realm unattended for too long risks destabilizing the Law itself. 
 
3. The Realm Spirits 
Each Realm possesses a guardian. 
These are known as Realm Spirits. 
Realm Spirits are not the Primordials. 
They are manifestations born from the Realm itself. 
Originally, they existed to help maintain balance and prevent corruption within their respective Laws. 
Over thousands of years, however, they absorbed the instability caused by humanity's repeated interference with 
reality. 
Instead of protecting balance, they gradually became twisted reflections of their own Laws. 
For example: 
• 
The Spirit of Time no longer values the natural flow of time—it seeks absolute control over every moment. 
• 
The Spirit of Life refuses to let anything die, creating endless suffering. 
• 
The Spirit of Death believes all existence should end equally. 
• 
The Spirit of Chaos rejects all order, believing uncertainty should consume reality. 
Each Spirit represents what happens when a Fundamental Law loses its balance. 
 
Developer Note 
The Realm Spirits are the game's primary bosses. 
Unlike the Primordials, they are meant to be defeated. 
Each boss should demonstrate both the beauty and the danger of its respective Law. 
 
4. The Seven Trials 
Christ cannot simply walk into a Primordial's Realm. 
Every Realm rejects those who are unworthy. 
To enter, he must overcome the Realm Spirit. 
Each Spirit tests more than combat ability. 
Each challenges Christ's understanding of the Law it represents. 
Examples: 
Realm of Time 
The flow of time constantly changes. 
Enemies grow older or younger during battle. 
Destroyed bridges rebuild themselves. 
Finished puzzles undo themselves. 
The player must think beyond normal cause and effect. 
Realm of Space 
Distance becomes meaningless. 
Rooms rearrange themselves. 
Doors lead to unexpected places. 
The player must navigate impossible geometry. 
Realm of Life 
The environment continuously grows. 
Plants block paths. 
Enemies regenerate. 
The player must decide when preserving life creates greater suffering. 
Realm of Death 
The Realm is filled with lingering memories of those who have died. 
Some help. 
Some deceive. 
Some simply refuse to move on. 
The challenge is accepting loss rather than defeating it. 
Realm of Creation 
New structures appear constantly. 
Weapons and enemies evolve during battle. 
Nothing remains unfinished. 
The player learns that endless creation without restraint eventually becomes overwhelming. 
Realm of Destruction 
Everything slowly crumbles. 
Weapons break. 
Platforms collapse. 
The player learns that destruction is necessary—but excess destruction leaves nothing behind. 
Realm of Chaos 
Nothing behaves consistently. 
Enemy patterns constantly change. 
Controls occasionally shift. 
Maps transform. 
Random events become part of the gameplay itself. 
The player must adapt instead of memorizing. 
 
5. The Meeting After Every Trial 
After defeating a Realm Spirit, Christ does not immediately continue. 
Instead... 
the Primordial of that Realm appears. 
Christ initially believes this is the moment he has been waiting for. 
He draws the Black Sword. 
The Primordial never draws a weapon. 
Instead... 
they ask him a single question. 
Every Primordial asks something different. 
Not to judge his strength... 
but to understand his choices. 
Examples: 
Chronar 
"If you could return to yesterday... would you become a different person?" 
Morvex 
"Would you still hate death if no one you loved had ever died?" 
Elaria 
"Is saving everyone always the same as helping everyone?" 
These conversations are deliberately philosophical. 
The Primordials never reveal the full truth. 
They simply plant seeds of doubt. 
Each meeting leaves Christ more confused than before. 
Developer Note 
These encounters should never feel like exposition dumps. 
The Primordials answer very few questions. 
Instead, they encourage the player to question their own assumptions. 
 
6. Christ's Internal Conflict 
At the beginning of the journey, Christ sees the Seven as murderers. 
After clearing each Realm, that certainty begins to weaken. 
He notices something strange. 
The Primordials never attack him. 
They never attempt to stop his progress. 
Some even help him after difficult battles. 
This creates a conflict within Christ. 
If they truly wanted him dead... 
they could have killed him long ago. 
So why are they letting him continue? 
This question becomes the driving force of the middle act of the game. 
 
7. The Truth Waiting at the End 
By the time Christ clears the Seventh Realm... 
he has changed. 
He began his journey seeking revenge. 
Now he seeks answers. 
The Seven finally invite him to the place where everything began. 
The Core. 
For the first time... 
Christ stands before the source of reality itself. 
There... 
the complete truth about his parents... 
the Primordials... 
the Choice Wielders... 
and the endless cycle... 
will finally be revealed. 
But with that truth comes an impossible choice. 
Not whether to save humanity. 
But what humanity truly deserves. 
End of Volume II 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Seven Realms are manifestations of the Fundamental Laws and act as anchors for reality. 
• 
Each Realm contains a corrupted Realm Spirit that must be defeated to restore balance. 
• 
The Primordials are not the game's bosses; they serve as mentors, judges, and witnesses. 
• 
Every Realm is both a gameplay challenge and a philosophical lesson about the Law it represents. 
• 
Christ's certainty that the Seven are villains slowly begins to crumble. 
• 
By the end of Volume II, Christ reaches the Core—not as a vengeful son, but as someone searching for the 
truth. 
 
 
Volume III – The Pilgrimage Through the Seven Realms 
Part 1 – The Realm of Time 
Purpose of this Part 
This section begins Christ's pilgrimage through the Seven Realms. 
The Realm of Time serves as the player's first major test. Rather than simply introducing a boss fight, it teaches the 
player that every Realm is a lesson about a Fundamental Law. 
This is also the first time Christ meets one of the Seven Primordials face-to-face. 
 
1. Why the Realm of Time Comes First 
The Seven Realms are not arranged randomly. 
Each Realm prepares Christ for the next. 
The Realm of Time always appears first because every journey begins with time. 
Without understanding the value of time, none of the other Laws can truly be understood. 
Chronar intentionally designed his Realm this way. 
Not to make it easier... 
but to make every future lesson meaningful. 
 
Developer Note 
The order of the Realms should never feel arbitrary. 
Every Law naturally leads into the next, creating both gameplay and narrative progression. 
 
2. Arrival 
The entrance to the Realm of Time appears beneath the ruins of an ancient observatory. 
As Christ approaches, the Black Sword begins vibrating. 
The previous Choice Wielders become unusually quiet. 
One finally speaks. 
"Every one of us began here." 
The gateway opens. 
Christ steps through. 
Immediately... 
the sky changes. 
The sun rises. 
Sets. 
Rises again. 
Entire days pass within seconds. 
Then everything freezes. 
Time no longer behaves consistently. 
 
3. Environmental Design 
The Realm of Time should feel beautiful... 
and unsettling. 
Examples include: 
• 
Forests rapidly aging into dead wastelands before blooming again.  
• 
Rivers flowing backward.  
• 
Buildings appearing newly constructed before instantly collapsing into ruins.  
• 
Birds flying in reverse.  
• 
Rain stopping in midair before continuing to fall.  
The player should constantly feel that the world itself refuses to obey normal rules. 
 
Developer Note 
Time distortion should affect gameplay as much as visuals. 
Players should never feel completely comfortable. 
 
4. Core Gameplay Mechanics 
Throughout the Realm, time continuously shifts. 
Mechanics include: 
Accelerated Time 
Plants instantly grow into climbable vines. 
Bridges decay and collapse. 
Enemies rapidly age into weaker forms. 
 
Reversed Time 
Destroyed mechanisms repair themselves. 
Broken doors become intact. 
Certain enemies return after defeat. 
 
Frozen Time 
Projectiles stop midair. 
Moving platforms become stationary. 
Water becomes solid. 
The player can use frozen moments to solve puzzles. 
 
Fractured Time 
Different areas operate at different speeds simultaneously. 
One room may be completely frozen while another moves hundreds of times faster. 
Players must understand the flow of each area rather than relying on instinct. 
 
5. The Spirit of Time 
At the center of the Realm waits the Spirit of Time. 
Unlike Chronar, the Spirit believes time should never change. 
It seeks perfect order. 
Perfect predictability. 
To it... 
every unexpected event is a flaw. 
It attempts to trap Christ inside repeating timelines where every decision leads to the same outcome. 
Its philosophy is simple: 
"If nothing changes... 
nothing can go wrong." 
This represents Time taken to its absolute extreme. 
 
Boss Mechanics 
The battle itself constantly changes. 
• 
The arena rewinds after certain attacks.  
• 
Defeated enemies return.  
• 
Christ's previous actions are replayed as ghostly echoes that can either help or hinder him.  
• 
Health lost during one phase may return in another as time reverses.  
Victory requires adapting rather than memorizing attack patterns. 
 
6. Defeating the Spirit 
Upon defeat, the Spirit does not explode or disappear. 
Instead... 
it smiles. 
For the first time, its rigid expression softens. 
"Time was never meant to imprison anyone." 
Its body dissolves into countless fragments of light. 
Those fragments spread across the Realm. 
Immediately... 
Time begins flowing naturally again. 
The Realm heals. 
This reinforces an important idea: 
Christ is not destroying the Realms. 
He is saving them. 
 
7. The First Meeting with a Primordial 
The Realm becomes silent. 
A single figure appears. 
Chronar. 
He carries no weapon. 
His face reflects centuries of exhaustion. 
Christ immediately raises the Black Sword. 
Chronar simply looks at him. 
Then asks: 
"Which moment made you believe revenge could heal grief?" 
Christ hesitates. 
Instead of answering, 
he demands the truth about his parents. 
Chronar refuses. 
Not because he wishes to hide the truth. 
Because Christ is not yet ready to understand it. 
Before disappearing, Chronar says: 
"Every answer has its proper time. 
Reach the end of your journey... 
then judge us." 
Chronar vanishes. 
Christ leaves frustrated. 
Yet... 
for the first time... 
he wonders why someone capable of killing him chose not to fight. 
 
8. Narrative Purpose 
The Realm of Time establishes the structure of the entire game. 
Each Realm follows the same progression: 
• 
Enter the Realm.  
• 
Learn its gameplay mechanics.  
• 
Discover fragments of lore.  
• 
Defeat the corrupted Realm Spirit.  
• 
Speak with the Primordial.  
• 
Leave with more questions than answers.  
The player's understanding of the world grows alongside Christ's. 
 
End of Part 1 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Realm of Time introduces the philosophy behind the Seven Realms.  
• 
Realm Spirits are corrupted manifestations of their Laws and must be freed, not simply defeated.  
• 
Chronar chooses conversation over combat, immediately challenging Christ's belief that the Primordials are 
monsters.  
• 
Every Realm combines gameplay, environmental storytelling, and philosophical themes.  
• 
Christ completes his first trial, but leaves with more doubts than certainty.  
 
Part 2 – The Realms of Space and Life 
 
Purpose of this Part 
This section covers Christ's journey through the second and third Realms—Space and Life. 
These Realms are designed to shift Christ's perspective. Until now, he believes strength is enough to complete his 
mission. These two Realms begin teaching him that understanding a Law is far more important than defeating it. 
The player should also begin questioning whether the Primordials are truly the villains of the story. 
 
1. Between the Realms 
After restoring the Realm of Time, Christ notices a change within the Black Sword. 
The voices become calmer. 
The previous Choice Wielders no longer constantly argue. 
Instead, they begin discussing the choices Christ makes. 
Some praise his compassion. 
Others criticize his hesitation. 
For the first time, Christ realizes the Sword is not judging him. 
It is observing him. 
Every decision he makes becomes part of the legacy that future Choice Wielders may one day inherit. 
 
Developer Note 
From this point onward, player choices should subtly influence dialogue from the Black Sword. 
Even though the main story remains the same, the Sword should remember whether the player solved situations 
through mercy, aggression, sacrifice, or pragmatism. 
This makes the player feel that their philosophy is becoming part of the Sword's history. 
 
2. The Realm of Space 
The entrance to the second Realm lies inside an abandoned temple that appears much larger on the inside than the 
outside. 
As Christ steps through the gateway, distance loses meaning. 
There is no horizon. 
No fixed direction. 
The sky folds into itself. 
Mountains float upside down. 
Entire islands drift through empty space. 
Roads twist into impossible shapes. 
Every location exists... 
yet none remain where they should. 
 
3. Philosophy of Space 
Most people believe Space is simply distance. 
Vastara understands something deeper. 
Space is what allows everything else to exist. 
Without space... 
there is nowhere for life to grow. 
Nowhere for time to flow. 
Nowhere for creation to expand. 
Without space... 
existence has no room to breathe. 
 
4. Gameplay Mechanics 
The Realm of Space focuses on navigation and perspective. 
Major mechanics include: 
Impossible Architecture 
Buildings connect to themselves. 
Rooms exist inside other rooms. 
Staircases loop endlessly. 
The player must observe patterns instead of relying on instinct. 
 
Dimensional Portals 
Certain doors lead to completely different parts of the Realm. 
The same doorway may lead somewhere different depending on the player's actions. 
 
Perspective Puzzles 
Objects appear different depending on where the player stands. 
A tiny bridge viewed from one angle becomes a massive pathway from another. 
The Realm constantly challenges the player's perception. 
 
Gravity Shifts 
Walking on walls. 
Walking across ceilings. 
Changing gravity to solve environmental puzzles. 
Combat also adapts to these changing orientations. 
 
Developer Note 
The Realm of Space should feel mysterious rather than dangerous. 
Players should frequently stop simply to admire the environment. 
Wonder is the primary emotion here. 
 
5. The Spirit of Space 
The Spirit believes every corner of reality should be perfectly organized. 
Nothing should ever move. 
Nothing should ever change location. 
Everything belongs exactly where it already exists. 
To the Spirit... 
freedom creates disorder. 
Its philosophy is: 
"A perfect world has no uncertainty." 
The Spirit attempts to imprison Christ inside endless geometric structures where every path eventually returns to the 
beginning. 
 
6. Meeting Vastara 
After defeating the Spirit, reality begins unfolding naturally again. 
Floating islands return to stable positions. 
Gravity normalizes. 
The Realm breathes once more. 
Vastara appears. 
She watches Christ quietly. 
Then asks: 
"If you could place every person exactly where they belong... 
would anyone still be free?" 
Christ immediately answers: 
"No." 
Vastara smiles for the first time. 
Without another word... 
she disappears. 
This is the first Primordial who neither challenges nor criticizes him. 
Instead... 
she allows him to discover the answer himself. 
 
7. The Realm of Life 
The third Realm feels completely different. 
There are no ruins. 
No destruction. 
No darkness. 
Everything is alive. 
Massive trees stretch beyond the clouds. 
Flowers bloom with every step. 
Creatures of every shape coexist peacefully. 
At first... 
the Realm appears perfect. 
Too perfect. 
 
8. Philosophy of Life 
Life exists because it changes. 
Birth. 
Growth. 
Decay. 
Death. 
Renewal. 
These are not separate events. 
They are one continuous cycle. 
The Spirit of Life forgot this truth. 
It came to believe that preserving life at any cost was true kindness. 
That belief slowly corrupted the Realm. 
 
9. Gameplay Mechanics 
The Realm of Life focuses on growth and adaptation. 
Major mechanics include: 
Living Environment 
Plants constantly grow. 
Roots create bridges. 
Flowers reveal hidden paths. 
Trees reshape the landscape over time. 
 
Symbiotic Creatures 
Not every enemy attacks. 
Some creatures assist the player. 
Others react differently depending on how Christ treats them. 
This introduces moral gameplay choices beyond combat. 
 
Regeneration 
Many enemies continuously heal. 
Victory requires understanding their behavior rather than simply dealing more damage. 
 
Ecosystem Puzzles 
Removing one plant may unintentionally kill another. 
Saving one creature may endanger another. 
The player learns that every form of life is interconnected. 
 
Developer Note 
Unlike previous Realms, violence should rarely be the optimal solution here. 
Encouraging peaceful interactions reinforces the Realm's philosophy. 
 
10. The Spirit of Life 
The Spirit believes death is always evil. 
It endlessly revives everything. 
Nothing is allowed to die. 
As a result... 
The Realm becomes overcrowded. 
Plants suffocate one another. 
Animals compete endlessly for diminishing resources. 
Living beings continue existing long after unbearable suffering should have ended. 
Life without death becomes its own form of cruelty. 
The Spirit cannot understand this. 
 
11. Meeting Elaria 
After restoring balance, the endless growth begins slowing naturally. 
The Realm finally breathes again. 
Elaria appears among blooming flowers. 
Unlike the other Primordials... 
she looks genuinely happy to see Christ alive. 
She asks only one question. 
"Would you save every life... 
even if saving one meant condemning thousands?" 
Christ cannot answer. 
For the first time... 
he remains completely silent. 
Elaria nods. 
"Good. 
Some questions should never have easy answers." 
She disappears. 
 
12. Christ Begins to Doubt 
Three Realms. 
Three Primordials. 
None have attacked him. 
None have defended themselves. 
None have justified killing his parents. 
Instead... 
they continue asking questions. 
Questions that force Christ to examine his own beliefs. 
For the first time... 
he wonders whether revenge is enough. 
The hatred remains. 
But certainty begins to fade. 
 
End of Part 2 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Black Sword evolves alongside Christ, recording not just his actions but the philosophy behind them. 
• 
The Realm of Space teaches that freedom requires uncertainty and that perfect order becomes a prison. 
• 
The Realm of Life demonstrates that preserving life without accepting death ultimately creates suffering. 
• 
Vastara and Elaria continue the Primordials' pattern of guiding Christ through questions rather than battles. 
• 
Christ begins to recognize that the Seven are not behaving like the monsters he imagined. 
• 
His journey gradually shifts from one of revenge to one of understanding, laying the groundwork for the 
emotional and philosophical conflicts that will dominate the remainder of the game. 
 
Part 3 – Death, Chaos, and the Cracks in Memory 
 
Purpose of this Part 
This section marks the emotional midpoint of the game. 
Christ enters the Realms of Death and Chaos, expecting to become stronger. Instead, both Realms attack something 
far more dangerous than his body—his certainty. 
By the end of this part, Christ no longer fully trusts his own memories. 
 
1. The Realm of Death 
Unlike the previous Realms, the Realm of Death is completely silent. 
No wind. 
No animals. 
No enemies waiting in ambush. 
Only an endless white landscape stretching toward the horizon. 
At first glance... 
it appears empty. 
But as Christ walks farther... 
figures begin appearing in the distance. 
They are people. 
Some wave at him. 
Some cry. 
Some simply watch. 
Every one of them is already dead. 
 
Developer Note 
The Realm of Death should not feel frightening. 
It should feel peaceful. 
The goal is to challenge the player's assumptions about death itself. 
 
2. The Weight of Memory 
The souls within the Realm are not trapped. 
They remain only because they have unfinished emotions. 
Some regret never saying goodbye. 
Some still wait for loved ones. 
Some cannot forgive themselves. 
Others cannot forgive the living. 
Christ slowly realizes something disturbing. 
Death is not suffering. 
Regret is. 
 
3. Gameplay Mechanics 
Instead of combat-heavy gameplay, the Realm focuses on emotional encounters. 
Players help souls move on by resolving their lingering regrets. 
Examples include: 
• 
Returning forgotten memories. 
• 
Delivering final messages. 
• 
Helping two spirits reconcile after centuries. 
• 
Allowing someone to finally accept their own death. 
Every soul that finds peace restores a fragment of the Realm. 
 
Developer Note 
This Realm intentionally slows the pace of the game. 
Players should spend time reflecting rather than fighting. 
 
4. The Spirit of Death 
At the Realm's center waits the Spirit of Death. 
Unlike the peaceful souls around it, the Spirit has become convinced that existence itself is suffering. 
Its conclusion is simple: 
"If no one lives... 
no one will ever grieve." 
This is Death without compassion. 
Not balance. 
Not acceptance. 
Only oblivion. 
The battle reflects this philosophy. 
The Spirit constantly drains Christ's strength, forcing him to survive through patience rather than aggression. 
 
5. Morvex 
After the Spirit is freed, the Realm becomes brighter. 
The wandering souls begin disappearing peacefully. 
Morvex appears. 
For the first time... 
he looks directly at Christ. 
His expression is filled with guilt. 
He asks only one question. 
"Do you hate me... 
because I brought death to your family... 
or because death reached them at all?" 
Christ cannot answer. 
Before leaving, Morvex quietly says: 
"One day... 
you'll know why I could never save them." 
Those words haunt Christ long after the Realm fades. 
 
6. The Realm of Chaos 
If the Realm of Death is quiet... 
the Realm of Chaos is overwhelming. 
Every step changes the world. 
Mountains melt into oceans. 
Day becomes night. 
Gravity reverses without warning. 
Music changes constantly. 
Even dialogue occasionally rearranges itself. 
Nothing remains stable. 
 
7. Gameplay Mechanics 
Chaos challenges adaptation rather than skill. 
Examples include: 
• 
Enemy attack patterns constantly evolve. 
• 
Environmental hazards change every attempt. 
• 
Puzzle solutions are never identical twice. 
• 
Certain NPCs remember choices the player hasn't made yet, hinting that Chaos exists outside linear time. 
Players who rely only on memorization will struggle. 
Players who observe and adapt will succeed. 
 
8. The Spirit of Chaos 
The Spirit believes rules are prisons. 
Its philosophy is simple: 
"If everything changes... 
no one can ever control anyone." 
Initially, this seems like freedom. 
But Christ soon realizes that endless unpredictability makes trust impossible. 
Without some order... 
friendships cannot form. 
Promises lose meaning. 
Civilizations collapse. 
Chaos without balance becomes isolation. 
 
9. Xael 
Xael appears laughing. 
Not because he enjoys suffering. 
Because every conversation with Christ unfolds differently. 
He is the only Primordial who genuinely cannot predict what Christ will say next. 
Instead of asking a question... 
Xael asks Christ to choose. 
Two identical doors appear. 
"There is no correct answer. 
Choose." 
Christ selects one. 
Both doors lead to the same destination. 
Xael smiles. 
"The destination never mattered. 
Only the fact that you were free to choose." 
Then he disappears. 
 
10. The Cracks in Memory 
As Christ leaves the Realm of Chaos... 
the Black Sword suddenly reacts violently. 
Every previous Choice Wielder begins speaking at once. 
The voices overlap. 
Contradict each other. 
Argue. 
Scream. 
Then... 
everything stops. 
Christ sees something he has never seen before. 
Not a dream. 
Not a vision. 
A memory. 
His father... 
standing beside Morvex. 
Talking. 
Laughing. 
Like old friends. 
Christ immediately rejects the memory. 
It cannot be real. 
Morvex murdered his parents. 
Why would they ever smile at one another? 
The Sword offers no explanation. 
Only silence. 
 
11. The Beginning of Doubt 
For the first time since leaving home... 
Christ questions whether his memories are complete. 
Had someone altered them? 
Had he misunderstood them? 
Or... 
had he built his entire life around an incomplete truth? 
The player ends this section asking the same question. 
Were the Primordials ever the true enemy? 
Or has Christ been chasing the wrong target from the very beginning? 
 
End of Part 3 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Realm of Death teaches that death itself is not evil—unresolved regret is. 
• 
Morvex's words hint that he never wanted Christ's parents to die. 
• 
The Realm of Chaos reinforces that true freedom requires both choice and responsibility. 
• 
Xael subtly reveals the central theme of the game: choices matter because they are freely made, not 
because they always change the outcome. 
• 
The Black Sword reveals Christ's first undeniable contradiction: his father once stood beside Morvex as an 
ally. 
• 
Christ's certainty finally breaks, setting up the final stretch of the journey. 
 
Part 4 – Creation, Destruction, and the Road to the Core 
 
Purpose of this Part 
This section concludes Christ's journey through the Seven Realms. 
The final two Laws—Creation and Destruction—teach Christ the most important lesson of all: 
Neither creation nor destruction is evil. Both become dangerous only when they exist without balance. 
By the end of this volume, Christ has restored all Seven Realms, earned the respect of every Primordial, and finally 
reaches the place where reality itself was born—the Core. 
 
1. The Realm of Creation 
The Realm of Creation is unlike anything Christ has seen. 
There are no ruins. 
No signs of age. 
Everything is constantly being born. 
Entire mountains rise from the earth. 
Rivers carve themselves into existence. 
Stars form overhead before fading into daylight. 
New plants bloom every second. 
New creatures emerge from light itself. 
The Realm never stops creating. 
At first... 
it feels beautiful. 
Then... 
it becomes overwhelming. 
Eventually, there is no room left for anything. 
New forests crush old ones. 
New mountains bury ancient valleys. 
New life replaces life that already exists. 
Creation has become endless. 
 
Philosophy of Creation 
Creation is hope. 
Every invention... 
every child... 
every dream... 
every beginning... 
exists because something new was allowed to exist. 
But creation without restraint becomes consumption. 
If nothing ever ends... 
nothing new truly has meaning. 
 
2. Gameplay Mechanics 
The Realm constantly generates new environments. 
No area remains identical for long. 
Examples: 
• 
Bridges grow while the player is crossing them. 
• 
Weapons evolve with continued use. 
• 
New enemies are born during combat. 
• 
Entire puzzle layouts regenerate if the player waits too long. 
Players are encouraged to keep moving forward rather than remaining comfortable. 
 
3. The Spirit of Creation 
The Spirit believes creating more is always better. 
Its philosophy is simple: 
"If something can exist... 
then it should." 
It refuses to allow anything to disappear. 
The battlefield itself continuously creates obstacles, weapons and enemies faster than Christ can destroy them. 
Victory comes not from destroying everything... 
but from finding the single source fueling endless creation. 
When that source is purified... 
the Realm finally becomes peaceful. 
 
4. Meeting Aetherion 
Aetherion appears surrounded by countless stars. 
Unlike the others... 
he smiles warmly. 
He asks Christ: 
"If you were given the power to create a perfect world... 
would you erase the imperfect one?" 
Christ remains silent. 
Aetherion nods. 
"Good. 
A perfect world built upon forgotten lives is not perfection. 
It is abandonment." 
As he fades away, he quietly says: 
"The Core has been waiting for you." 
Those words stay with Christ. 
For the first time... 
one of the Primordials sounds hopeful. 
 
5. The Realm of Destruction 
The final Realm is terrifying. 
Everything is collapsing. 
Entire cities crumble into dust. 
Mountains split apart. 
The sky itself cracks. 
Nothing remains whole. 
Yet... 
from the ruins... 
small flowers begin growing. 
Life emerges where destruction has already passed. 
Christ begins to understand. 
Destruction does not erase. 
It makes room. 
 
Philosophy of Destruction 
Every ending creates the possibility of another beginning. 
Without destruction... 
old ideas never disappear. 
Old civilizations never evolve. 
Old mistakes remain forever. 
Destruction exists to prevent stagnation. 
 
6. Gameplay Mechanics 
Unlike previous Realms, resources are limited. 
Weapons deteriorate. 
Platforms collapse permanently. 
Safe zones disappear over time. 
Players must decide carefully: 
• 
Which paths to preserve. 
• 
Which bridges to sacrifice. 
• 
Which tools deserve repairing. 
• 
Which battles are worth fighting. 
The Realm constantly asks: 
"What are you willing to let go?" 
 
7. The Spirit of Destruction 
The Spirit has forgotten balance. 
It believes everything eventually deserves destruction. 
Its philosophy is: 
"If all things end... 
why preserve anything?" 
It constantly destroys the arena itself. 
Pieces of the battlefield disappear. 
Entire sections collapse into darkness. 
Victory comes only after Christ refuses to fight endlessly. 
Instead... 
he protects what remains. 
The Spirit finally understands. 
Destruction exists to create possibility... 
not emptiness. 
It peacefully dissolves. 
 
8. Nihros 
The final Primordial appears. 
Nihros. 
The one humanity feared most. 
He carries enormous scars across his body. 
Not from battle. 
From reality itself. 
Every necessary destruction leaves its mark upon him. 
He asks Christ: 
"Tell me... 
what deserves to survive?" 
Christ begins answering... 
then stops. 
After everything he has witnessed... 
he no longer knows. 
Nihros smiles. 
For the first time in thousands of years. 
"Then you've finally begun to understand." 
 
9. The Seven Gather 
As Christ exits the final Realm... 
something unprecedented happens. 
Instead of disappearing individually... 
all Seven Primordials stand together. 
Not surrounding him. 
Standing beside one another. 
They do not bow. 
Neither does Christ. 
For several moments... 
no one speaks. 
Then Chronar breaks the silence. 
"Every Choice Wielder reached this point believing they understood reality." 
He looks directly into Christ's eyes. 
"You are the first to arrive knowing you do not." 
The Seven simultaneously open a gateway. 
Beyond it lies a place unlike any Realm. 
A place older than Time. 
Older than Creation. 
Older than the Primordials themselves. 
The Core. 
 
10. The Last Voice 
Before Christ enters... 
the Black Sword speaks one final time. 
For the first time... 
every previous Choice Wielder falls silent. 
Only one voice remains. 
His father's. 
Christ finally recognizes it. 
"I've guided you as far as I could." 
Christ freezes. 
"...Father?" 
The voice continues. 
"Do not enter the Core seeking revenge." 
A pause. 
"Do not enter seeking forgiveness." 
Another pause. 
"Enter seeking the truth." 
The voice disappears. 
Forever. 
The Sword becomes completely silent. 
Its purpose has been fulfilled. 
From this point onward... 
every decision belongs solely to Christ. 
 
11. The End of the Journey 
Christ looks back one last time. 
The Seven Realms stand restored. 
The Primordials wait patiently. 
No enemies remain. 
No battles lie ahead. 
Only one question. 
What truly caused the endless cycle? 
Christ steps through the gateway. 
The entrance closes behind him. 
 
End of Volume III 
Key Takeaways 
• 
Christ restores the Realms of Creation and Destruction, completing his pilgrimage through all Seven Realms. 
• 
He learns that every Fundamental Law becomes destructive when separated from balance. 
• 
Each Primordial acknowledges Christ's growth, not because he has become stronger, but because he has 
become wiser. 
• 
The Seven reveal the path to the Core willingly, proving they never intended to stop him. 
• 
The Black Sword fulfills its purpose, and the spirit of Christ's father finally reveals himself before departing. 
• 
Christ enters the Core without certainty, revenge, or hatred—only with a desire to uncover the complete 
truth. 
 
 
Volume IV – The Core and the Final Choice 
Part 1 – The Truth of the Core 
 
Purpose of this Part 
This section reveals the complete truth behind reality. 
Christ finally learns why the Seven became Primordials, why the Choice Wielders always failed, why his parents 
sacrificed themselves, and what the Core actually is. 
Most importantly, it reveals that the Core is not the villain. 
It is merely doing exactly what it was created to do. 
 
1. The Heart of Reality 
The Core is unlike anything Christ imagined. 
There are no walls. 
No sky. 
No floor. 
It exists outside Time, Space, Life, and every other Fundamental Law. 
It appears as an endless sea of white light, with fragments of memories floating like stars. 
Every memory belongs to someone. 
Some belong to ordinary humans. 
Others belong to the Primordials. 
Others belong to Choice Wielders who lived thousands of years ago. 
The Core does not speak. 
It remembers. 
 
Developer Note 
The Core should feel sacred, not threatening. 
The player should feel as though they have entered a place no mortal was ever meant to see. 
 
2. The First Memory 
As Christ steps forward, the first memory surrounds him. 
He no longer watches history. 
He lives it. 
He sees humanity before the Seven existed. 
Cities filled with brilliant inventors. 
Artists. 
Scientists. 
Philosophers. 
People capable of incredible kindness... 
and incredible cruelty. 
Humanity flourished. 
Then ambition became greed. 
Kings sought immortality. 
Empires sought absolute control. 
Scholars attempted to command Time itself. 
Others tried to erase Death. 
Some wished to create endless life. 
Others wanted limitless destruction. 
The Fundamental Laws were treated as tools. 
Eventually... 
humanity discovered the Core. 
 
3. The Original Catastrophe 
The Core did not grant wishes. 
It simply amplified intent. 
When humanity attempted to rewrite reality, the Core obeyed. 
Reality fractured. 
Time splintered. 
Space collapsed. 
Life and Death lost balance. 
Creation became uncontrollable. 
Chaos spread across existence. 
Entire civilizations disappeared in moments. 
The world nearly ended. 
This became the First Collapse. 
 
4. The Volunteers 
As the First Collapse consumed reality, seven ordinary humans stepped forward. 
Not kings. 
Not gods. 
Not chosen heroes. 
Simply people willing to sacrifice everything. 
Each accepted responsibility for stabilizing one Fundamental Law. 
Not because they desired power... 
but because someone had to. 
The Core accepted their decision. 
From that moment onward... 
they ceased to be ordinary humans. 
They became the Seven Primordials. 
 
5. The Price of Becoming a Primordial 
The Core demanded one condition. 
A Fundamental Law could never belong to someone seeking personal happiness. 
The Seven surrendered everything. 
They could never grow old. 
They could never abandon their Law. 
They could never truly live as humans again. 
Their names became part of reality itself. 
Their emotions slowly faded beneath endless responsibility. 
The burden they accepted was never meant to be temporary. 
It was eternal. 
 
6. Why the Choice Wielders Existed 
After restoring reality, the Seven immediately understood something horrifying. 
Reality was stable. 
But it was not healthy. 
Humanity still possessed free will. 
One day... 
someone would attempt to control the Core again. 
The Seven could prevent disasters. 
But they could not remove humanity's ability to choose. 
Doing so would destroy what made humanity human. 
They needed another solution. 
Not rulers. 
Not soldiers. 
Observers. 
People capable of understanding both humanity and the Primordials. 
Thus began the cycle of the Choice Wielders. 
Each generation would inherit the Black Sword. 
Each would search for a better future. 
Not because the Seven expected success... 
but because they refused to abandon hope. 
 
7. Why Every Choice Wielder Failed 
The memories continue. 
Christ watches every previous Choice Wielder. 
Some tried destroying the Core. 
Reality immediately began collapsing. 
Some tried replacing the Primordials. 
They slowly became prisoners of the Laws themselves. 
Others attempted to free humanity completely. 
Humanity repeated the mistakes that caused the First Collapse. 
Every solution saved one part of reality while destroying another. 
There was never a perfect answer. 
 
8. His Parents' Choice 
The final memory appears. 
Christ watches his parents. 
Not as victims. 
As two people making an impossible decision. 
His father says: 
"Every Choice Wielder inherited thousands of years of failure." 
His mother replies: 
"Then perhaps hope should be born instead of inherited." 
They both know what the Seven will do. 
Neither cries. 
Neither hesitates. 
His father kneels beside the newborn Christ. 
"If we are wrong... 
forgive us." 
The memory fades. 
For the first time... 
Christ understands. 
His parents did not die because they were unlucky. 
They chose that fate. 
Because they believed he represented humanity's final chance to break the cycle. 
 
9. The Core Speaks 
For the first time in history... 
the Core communicates. 
Not with words. 
With understanding. 
Christ suddenly realizes the truth. 
The Core has never manipulated anyone. 
Never judged anyone. 
Never chosen heroes. 
It simply responds to the choices freely made by living beings. 
It has no desires. 
No morality. 
No ambition. 
It is a mirror. 
The world has always reflected the choices of those who shape it. 
 
10. Christ's Realization 
Everything he believed was incomplete. 
The Seven were not tyrants. 
His parents were not helpless victims. 
The Choice Wielders were not failures. 
Even humanity was not purely evil. 
Everyone had simply been trying to protect what they believed was right. 
The true enemy... 
was the endless cycle forcing every generation to repeat the same impossible decision. 
Christ finally understands why he exists. 
Not to defeat the Seven. 
Not to destroy humanity. 
Not even to destroy the Core. 
He exists because he is the first person born outside the cycle. 
The first person capable of making a choice that was never available to those before him. 
 
End of Part 1 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Core is revealed as the memory and mirror of reality, not its ruler.  
• 
The Seven were ordinary humans who willingly accepted eternal burdens to save existence during the First 
Collapse.  
• 
The Core never grants power based on worthiness; it simply responds to genuine choices.  
• 
The Choice Wielders were created to seek a solution beyond the Seven's perspective, but every one of them 
ultimately failed.  
• 
Christ's parents knowingly sacrificed themselves so that someone could be born carrying Choice instead of 
inheriting it later in life.  
• 
Christ discovers that his purpose is not revenge, but to attempt the one thing no one before him could: break 
the cycle itself.  
 
Part 2 – The Burden of the Seven 
 
Purpose of this Part 
This section is entirely emotional. The player already knows the truth about history; now they learn the truth about 
the Seven themselves. 
Instead of explaining what the Primordials did, this chapter shows what it cost them. 
By the end of this part, Christ no longer sees them as gods. 
He sees them as seven exhausted people who have carried reality on their shoulders for ten thousand years. 
 
1. The Seven Enter the Core 
One by one... 
the Seven walk into the Core. 
Not as rulers. 
Not as judges. 
Simply as seven ordinary people. 
For the first time... 
their divine appearances disappear. 
Their crowns fade. 
Their robes vanish. 
Their immense power dissolves into white light. 
Standing before Christ are the same seven humans who volunteered during the First Collapse. 
Older. 
Tired. 
Broken. 
Yet still standing. 
 
2. The Weight They Carried 
The Core reveals their memories. 
Not grand battles. 
Not heroic victories. 
Just ten thousand years of endless responsibility. 
 
Chronar – Time 
Every second that passes... 
Chronar experiences it. 
Not one timeline. 
Every possible timeline. 
He has watched Christ die millions of times. 
He has watched civilizations rise and disappear. 
He remembers every conversation ever spoken. 
He cannot forget anything. 
Sleep no longer exists for him. 
For ten thousand years... 
he has never experienced silence. 
 
Vastara – Space 
Vastara feels every point in existence simultaneously. 
Every collapsing star. 
Every newborn world. 
Every distant galaxy. 
Every empty corner of reality. 
She has never known what loneliness means. 
Because she has never truly been alone. 
 
Elaria – Life 
Every birth fills her with joy. 
Every illness hurts her. 
Every dying child makes her cry. 
Every flower blooming makes her smile. 
She experiences every living thing. 
Every moment. 
Forever. 
 
Morvex – Death 
Every death passes through him. 
Not only humans. 
Every animal. 
Every insect. 
Every civilization. 
Every star. 
He personally escorts every soul toward peace. 
He has cried more tears than all of humanity combined. 
Yet... 
he must never refuse anyone. 
 
Nihros – Destruction 
Every necessary ending scars him. 
Every civilization that must fall. 
Every mountain that must crumble. 
Every dying sun. 
He destroys not because he enjoys it... 
but because new beginnings require space. 
Every destruction leaves another scar upon his body. 
 
Aetherion – Creation 
Every new possibility begins with him. 
Every dream. 
Every invention. 
Every hope. 
Yet he knows... 
many of those creations will eventually become weapons. 
Still... 
he continues creating. 
Because hope is always worth another chance. 
 
Xael – Chaos 
Chaos constantly changes. 
Xael has no stable identity. 
Every thought alters him. 
Every possibility reshapes his existence. 
Sometimes he cannot even remember his own face. 
He laughs... 
because laughter is the only thing that remains constant. 
 
3. Why They Never Chose Successors 
Christ finally asks the question that has haunted him since childhood. 
"If your burden is so terrible... 
why didn't you simply give it to someone else?" 
Chronar answers. 
"We could have." 
Silence. 
"But then we would become exactly what humanity once feared." 
Another pause. 
"We chose this burden. 
No one else should inherit it without making that same choice." 
Christ suddenly remembers the oldest law. 
No Fundamental Law may pass through blood. 
Now... 
he finally understands why. 
 
4. The Truth About His Parents 
Morvex steps forward. 
For thousands of years... 
this is the conversation he dreaded most. 
"I did not kill your parents because I hated them." 
He lowers his head. 
"I killed them because they asked me to." 
Christ freezes. 
Morvex continues. 
"They understood what breaking the law would mean." 
"They refused to run." 
"They refused to fight." 
"They accepted death... 
so that you would never have to carry guilt for their decision." 
Christ cannot believe it. 
Then... 
the Core shows the final memory. 
His parents kneeling before the Seven. 
His father speaks. 
"Don't make an exception because we're your friends." 
His mother smiles. 
"If the law survives because of our deaths... 
then let our deaths mean something." 
The Seven didn't execute them out of anger. 
They honored their final request. 
 
5. Why Christ Was Left Alive 
Aetherion finally answers the question Christ had carried since childhood. 
"We never spared you because you were special." 
"We spared you... 
because you had never chosen anything." 
Choice. 
That was always the point. 
The Seven refused to decide Christ's future. 
Only Christ himself could do that. 
 
6. The Burden Ends 
For the first time in ten thousand years... 
the Seven kneel. 
Not in worship. 
Not in surrender. 
In relief. 
Chronar speaks. 
"We have reached the end of our responsibility." 
Morvex quietly closes his eyes. 
"We protected reality until someone capable of making a new choice arrived." 
Elaria smiles through tears. 
"You." 
None of them ask Christ to replace them. 
None ask him to save them. 
For the first time... 
they ask nothing. 
 
7. The Final Truth 
The Core finally reveals the one truth it had hidden. 
The cycle was never created by humanity. 
Nor by the Seven. 
Nor by the Choice Wielders. 
It was created by the Core itself. 
Not intentionally. 
The Core simply mirrors choices. 
Every Choice Wielder inherited the memories of previous ones. 
Every generation carried the expectations of the last. 
The more memories accumulated... 
the fewer genuine choices remained. 
The cycle repeated because no one was ever truly free. 
They inherited too much. 
Christ is different. 
Because he was born before receiving the Black Sword. 
He experienced love. 
Friendship. 
Loss. 
Curiosity. 
Life. 
Before inheriting ten thousand years of memories. 
For the first time... 
Choice belongs to someone whose identity wasn't shaped entirely by the past. 
 
8. One Last Question 
The Seven stand. 
The Core falls silent. 
Reality itself waits. 
Chronar asks the final question. 
Not as a Primordial. 
As a man. 
"Christ..." 
"If you could free us..." 
"...what would you choose?" 
The screen fades to white. 
No answer is given. 
The player has reached the point where the story no longer belongs to the writers. 
It belongs to them. 
 
End of Part 2 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Seven are revealed as ordinary humans who never stopped paying the price of their sacrifice. 
• 
Each Primordial's "power" is shown to be an eternal burden rather than a privilege. 
• 
Christ finally learns that his parents willingly accepted death to preserve the oldest law. 
• 
The Seven spared Christ because they believed that only a person who could make their own choices 
deserved to decide the future. 
• 
The endless cycle exists because every Choice Wielder inherited the weight of the past, making true freedom 
impossible. 
• 
Christ, having lived a genuine human life before inheriting the Black Sword, is the first Choice Wielder 
capable of making a truly independent decision. 
 
Part 3 – The Three Choices 
 
Purpose of this Part 
This chapter is the climax of the entire story. 
After learning the truth, Christ must make the greatest decision in history. There is no "good" or "bad" ending. Each 
ending reflects a different philosophy, and each comes with its own sacrifice. 
The player's choices throughout the game—how they treated NPCs, solved dilemmas, and responded to the 
Primordials' questions—unlock or influence these endings. 
 
1. The Final Chamber 
Beyond the Core lies a circular chamber unlike anything else in existence. 
There are no walls. 
Only an endless horizon of light. 
In the center floats the Core—not as a glowing orb, but as a constantly shifting sphere made from countless 
memories, voices, and possibilities. 
Around it stand the Seven Primordials. 
They do not move. 
They do not speak. 
For the first time in ten thousand years... 
they wait for someone else's decision. 
 
2. The Black Sword's Last Purpose 
The Black Sword begins breaking apart. 
Its black metal slowly turns into white fragments of light. 
Every previous Choice Wielder appears around Christ. 
Not as ghosts. 
As memories finally set free. 
Some smile proudly. 
Some still regret their choices. 
Some remain uncertain. 
One by one, they thank Christ. 
Not because he succeeded. 
But because he reached the place none of them truly understood. 
Finally... 
his father appears. 
He embraces Christ. 
No words. 
No grand speech. 
Just a father seeing his son one last time. 
He fades peacefully. 
The Black Sword completely disappears. 
The burden of every previous Choice Wielder ends here. 
From this point onward... 
Christ stands entirely alone. 
 
3. The Core Offers Three Paths 
The Core does not command Christ. 
Instead, it reveals three possibilities. 
Not prophecies. 
Not predetermined futures. 
Only possibilities. 
Each represents a different answer to the same question: 
"How should reality move forward?" 
 
Ending One – The Eternal Guardian 
Christ accepts the burden of the Seven. 
He absorbs the Fundamental Laws into himself. 
The Seven are finally released from ten thousand years of responsibility. 
For the first time, they become mortal again. 
They grow old. 
They laugh. 
They cry. 
They finally live the ordinary lives they sacrificed long ago. 
Christ remains alone within the Core. 
Immortal. 
Watching over reality. 
His parents' sacrifice ultimately leads to humanity's survival. 
But the price is absolute solitude. 
He becomes the final guardian. 
 
Theme 
Self-sacrifice. 
One life willingly bears the burden so that countless others may live freely. 
 
Ending Two – The Age of Humanity 
Christ destroys the Core. 
Without the Core, the Fundamental Laws are no longer anchored by a central force. 
Instead, they become part of the natural universe itself. 
The Primordials disappear peacefully. 
The Black Sword is gone forever. 
Humanity is finally left without gods, guardians, or Chosen Ones. 
The future becomes uncertain. 
Wars may happen. 
Peace may happen. 
Great civilizations may rise. 
Others may fall. 
For the first time... 
every future belongs entirely to humanity. 
The age of divine intervention ends. 
 
Theme 
Freedom. 
A future without destiny is both terrifying and beautiful. 
 
Ending Three – The Broken Cycle (True Ending) 
Instead of choosing either burden or destruction... 
Christ chooses something no previous Choice Wielder imagined. 
He separates responsibility. 
The Seven remain. 
But no longer alone. 
Instead of seven eternal guardians... 
the burden is shared among humanity itself. 
Every generation willingly contributes a tiny fragment of itself to sustaining reality. 
Not through blood. 
Not through inheritance. 
Through voluntary choice. 
Scientists. 
Artists. 
Farmers. 
Teachers. 
Children. 
Anyone may one day choose to become a temporary Keeper of a Law. 
No one serves forever. 
No one inherits the burden. 
No one is forced. 
Reality survives because everyone carries a small responsibility instead of one person carrying everything. 
The Seven finally step down. 
Not because they are replaced. 
But because humanity has finally matured enough to stand beside them. 
The cycle ends. 
Not through destruction. 
Not through sacrifice. 
Through cooperation. 
 
Theme 
Shared responsibility. 
The future is strongest when no one is expected to carry it alone. 
 
4. Epilogues 
Each ending includes a short epilogue showing the world's future. 
Eternal Guardian 
Generations pass. 
Legends speak of a silent figure watching over reality. 
No one remembers his name. 
But every sunrise exists because of his choice. 
 
Age of Humanity 
The world becomes unpredictable. 
Some centuries are peaceful. 
Others are filled with conflict. 
Yet every achievement... 
and every mistake... 
belongs entirely to humanity. 
The stars shine without anyone guiding them. 
 
Broken Cycle 
Years later... 
children study the story of the Seven not as gods... 
but as the first people who taught humanity that responsibility should be shared. 
The Primordials live among ordinary people as mentors. 
Christ finally visits his parents' grave. 
Not as a warrior. 
Not as a savior. 
Simply as their son. 
He smiles. 
For the first time since childhood. 
 
5. Final Scene 
Regardless of the ending... 
the final scene is always the same. 
A young child discovers an ordinary black flower growing alone. 
They pick it up. 
They look toward the horizon. 
Then smile. 
The screen fades to black. 
 
Final Narration 
"Reality was never shaped by power." 
"It was shaped by choices." 
"Some choices created worlds." 
"Some destroyed them." 
"But every future..." 
"...began with someone willing to choose." 
 
End of Part 3 
Key Takeaways 
• 
The Black Sword completes its purpose and releases every previous Choice Wielder. 
• 
Christ alone is entrusted with the fate of reality. 
• 
The player chooses one of three philosophical endings: sacrifice, freedom, or shared responsibility. 
• 
No ending is presented as objectively correct; each reflects a different worldview.

Part 4 – World Reference & Developer Bible 
 
Purpose of this Part 
Unlike the previous chapters, this section is not part of the story. It serves as a reference document for everyone 
working on the game—writers, artists, programmers, designers, and future contributors. 
Its purpose is to ensure that the game's world, rules, and lore remain internally consistent.

• 
He restores the Seven Realms, uncovers the truth, reaches the Core, and makes the Final Choice. 
 
II. Universal Laws of Reality 
These laws can never be broken without consequences. 
Law 1 – Every Fundamental Law requires a guardian. 
Without one, reality collapses. 
 
Law 2 – No guardian may pass their burden through blood. 
Every successor must freely choose the responsibility. 
 
Law 3 – The Core never forces destiny. 
It only reflects the choices of sentient beings. 
 
Law 4 – Every Fundamental Law must remain balanced. 
An excess of any Law corrupts reality. 
 
Law 5 – Every choice creates consequences. 
The Core amplifies consequences but never decides them.

V. The Black Sword 
The Black Sword is not an evil weapon. 
It serves three purposes: 
1. Preserve the memories of every Choice Wielder. 
2. Judge the philosophy of its current bearer. 
3. Guide humanity toward breaking the cycle. 
After the Final Choice, the Black Sword ceases to exist. 
Its purpose is fulfilled.

VII. Central Themes 
The story explores several philosophical ideas: 
• 
Revenge cannot heal grief. 
• 
Freedom requires responsibility. 
• 
Every beginning requires an ending. 
• 
Perfection is impossible. 
• 
The burden of protecting the world should never fall on one individual. 
• 
Humanity is capable of both incredible kindness and terrible destruction. 
• 
True choice can exist only when people are free from inherited expectations.

X. Future Expansion Opportunities 
This universe allows room for future stories without changing the main narrative. 
Possible expansions include: 
• 
Stories of previous Choice Wielders. 
• 
The First Collapse and the origins of the Seven. 
• 
Humanity's golden age before discovering the Core. 
• 
Individual stories set within each Realm. 
• 
A sequel set after the Broken Cycle ending, showing how humanity governs the Fundamental Laws together. 
 
This story was never intended to be about defeating gods. 
It is a story about understanding them. 
Every enemy exists because a single truth was taken to an unhealthy extreme. 
Every boss represents imbalance. 
Every Realm represents a lesson. 
Every Primordial represents sacrifice. 
Every Choice Wielder represents hope. 
And Christ represents the one thing that no cycle, no law, and no destiny can ever fully control— 
the freedom to choose.
