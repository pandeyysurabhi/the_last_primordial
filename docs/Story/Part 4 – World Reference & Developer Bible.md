Purpose of this Part
Unlike the previous chapters, this section is not part of the story. It serves as a reference document for everyone
working on the game—writers, artists, programmers, designers, and future contributors.

II. [[Universal Laws of Reality]]
These laws can never be broken without consequences.
Law 1 – Every Fundamental Law requires a guardian.
Without one, reality collapses.
Law 2 – No guardian may pass their burden through blood.
Every successor must freely choose the responsibility.
Law 3 – [[The Core]] never forces destiny.
It only reflects the choices of sentient beings.
Law 4 – Every Fundamental Law must remain balanced.
An excess of any Law corrupts reality.
Law 5 – Every [[Choice|choice]] creates consequences.
[[The Core]] amplifies consequences but never decides them.

V. [[The Black Sword]]
[[The Black Sword]] is not an evil weapon.
It serves three purposes:
1. Preserve the memories of every [[Choice Wielder]].
2. Judge the philosophy of its current bearer.
3. Guide humanity toward breaking the cycle.
After the Final [[Choice]], [[The Black Sword|the Black Sword]] ceases to exist.
Its purpose is fulfilled.

X. Future Expansion Opportunities
This universe allows room for future stories without changing the main narrative.
Possible expansions include:
• Stories of previous [[Choice Wielder|Choice Wielders]].
• The First Collapse and the origins of the Seven.
• Humanity's golden age before discovering [[The Core|the Core]].
• Individual stories set within each Realm.
• A sequel set after the Broken Cycle ending, showing how humanity governs the Fundamental Laws together.
This story was never intended to be about defeating gods.
It is a story about understanding them.
Every enemy exists because a single truth was taken to an unhealthy extreme.
Every boss represents imbalance.
Every Realm represents a lesson.
Every Primordial represents sacrifice.
Every [[Choice Wielder]] represents hope.
And [[Christ]] represents the one thing that no cycle, no law, and no destiny can ever fully control—

the freedom to choose.
The End.
