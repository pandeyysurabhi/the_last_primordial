# Part 3 – The Seven Primordials
**Parent Volume**: [[Volume I – The Birth of Reality]]

## Introduction
Purpose of this Part
This section explains how the Seven Volunteers became the Primordials, the sacrifices they made, the responsibilities
they inherited, and why they should never be viewed as gods or villains. It also establishes the emotional foundation
of the story by showing that the Primordials are victims of their own choices.

## Key Takeaways
•
•
•

The Seven became guardians, not rulers.
Immortality became an eternal punishment rather than a reward.
Each Primordial experiences constant suffering directly tied to their law.

•
•
•

Humanity gradually forgot the truth and began worshipping the Seven as gods.
The Seven realized humanity's greatest weakness was not greed, but the belief that reality could be
perfected.
They discovered the existence of an Eighth Law—[[Choice]]—which could never be permanently contained.
